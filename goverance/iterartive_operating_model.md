# Iterative Operating Model

This system is designed to mature over time.

The first version should evaluate quality performance across the company at an enterprise level. Later versions should break the analysis into specific leader-ready views by vertical, product type, brand, issue type, customer segment, channel, and business unit.

---

## Phase 1: Enterprise Quality View

Answer:

- What are the largest quality issues across the company?
- Which issues create the most cost, friction, claims, rework, delay, or customer dissatisfaction?
- Which products, processes, customers, brands, or channels drive the greatest business impact?
- Which opportunities have the strongest ROI case?

---

## Phase 2: Breakout by Business Dimension

Segment by:

- Vertical
- Product type
- Brand
- Customer segment
- Sales channel
- Region
- Business unit
- Failure type
- Claim type
- Process stage
- Strategic growth area

---

## Phase 3: Leader-Tailored Insights

Each leader should receive analysis that answers:

- What quality issues matter most to my business area?
- Which issues affect my customers?
- Which products or brands under my responsibility carry the most quality risk?
- Where are we losing margin, cash, or customer confidence?
- Which improvement opportunities should I prioritize?
- What actions should my team take next?

---

## Design Principle

The system should move from broad visibility to targeted decision support.

```text
Enterprise View → Segment View → Leader-Specific View → Actionable Improvement Plan
```
